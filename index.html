Your health & safety as a carer
To give good care, you must first be able to care for yourself
Fatigue impairs judgement, reaction time and emotional patience — all critical in a care setting
Medication errors and missed observations are significantly more likely when a carer is overworked
Compassion fatigue sets in gradually — often before a carer realises it is happening
The 8–10 hour standard protects both you and your client — it exists for a reason
Regular breaks, adequate sleep and time outside are professional requirements, not luxuries
Know your rights — and stand by them
Not covered by the Working Time Regulations — but you still have rights

Agree hours in writing before each placement starts. If a client or family asks you to work beyond what was agreed, you are entitled to decline or renegotiate. Your wellbeing is not separate from the quality of care you provide — it is part of it. A placement requiring more than 10 hours of active care per day should be documented clearly and reflected in your rate.

▼ accommodation offset reference
For reference only — not applicable to self-employed
The HMRC accommodation offset applies to employed workers only. It cannot be claimed as a tax deduction by self-employed carers. Shown here as a package comparison reference only.
