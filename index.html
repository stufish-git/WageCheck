Your health & safety as a carer
To give good care, you must first be able to care for yourself
Fatigue impairs judgement, reaction time and emotional patience — all critical in a care setting
Medication errors and missed observations are significantly more likely when a carer is overworked
Compassion fatigue sets in gradually — often before a carer realises it is happening
The 8–10 hour standard protects both you and your client — it exists for a reason
Regular breaks, adequate sleep and time outside the home are professional requirements, not luxuries
Know your rights — and stand by them
You may not have the same legal protections — but you still have rights

Although self-employed carers are not covered by the Working Time Regulations, you are still entitled to negotiate fair working arrangements. Agreeing hours in writing before a placement starts is your strongest protection. If a client or family asks you to work beyond what was agreed, you are entitled to decline or renegotiate your terms. Your wellbeing is not separate from the quality of care you provide — it is part of it. A placement requiring more than 10 hours of active care per day should be documented clearly and reflected in your rate. You have the right to set boundaries, take your breaks, and protect your health — for your sake and your client’s.
