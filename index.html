Know your rights as a self-employed carer
The accommodation offset cannot make up the shortfall

If an agency or client argues that free accommodation brings your pay above NLW — this does not hold. The HMRC accommodation offset is an employer/employee mechanism only. It has no legal application to self-employed workers.

Self-employed status and NMW rules cannot both apply

An agency cannot call you self-employed to avoid employer obligations, then use NMW offset rules to justify low pay. Those rules only exist within an employment relationship — they cannot use both positions at once.

You may actually be a worker, not self-employed

If you work set hours, for one client, directed by an agency — HMRC or an employment tribunal may decide you are a worker or employee in substance. If so, NMW applies and back pay can be claimed.

Where to get help

ACAS runs a free helpline for pay disputes. HMRC has a National Minimum Wage complaints process. Citizens Advice can help you understand your employment status and rights.
